NFPA 211 Inspection Protocols
🔍 Inspection Levels & Triggers
Level 1 Inspection
Readily accessible areas		 Routine annual maintenance Same appliance, same fuel Field Translation: You only do a Level 1 if absolutely nothing has changed with the system since the last sweep, and you are just doing a standard cleaning with a flashlight and brush.
Level 2 Inspection
Accessible + Camera Scan		 Sale/Transfer of property Post-chimney fire or seismic event Changing fuel type or relining Mandatory camera scan Field Translation: If someone just bought the house, had a chimney fire, or is swapping a wood stove for a gas log set, you MUST run a camera up the flue to check for hidden damage. No exceptions.
Defects & Flue Maintenance
🚫 Defect Limits & Failure Criteria
Creosote Buildup
Sweeping requirement		 Level 1/2 Sweep if > 1/8" thick Fails if Stage 3 (Glaze) is present Field Translation: If the buildup looks like shiny, hard, black glass (Stage 3), standard sweeping won't work. It is highly flammable. Fail it and quote a chemical rotary treatment (PCR).
Flue Liner Integrity
Terra cotta tile defects		 Zero tolerance for cracks/splits Joint gaps must not exceed 1/8" Fails if ANY tile is cracked Field Translation: Even a hairline crack in a clay tile means carbon monoxide or fire can escape into the home's framing. You must fail the flue and quote a stainless steel liner or HeatShield repair.
Relining & Terminations
🛡️ UL 1777 Stainless Steel Relining
Alloy Selection
Matches fuel type		 316Ti / 316L: Wood, Coal, Pellet, Oil AL29-4C: High-Efficiency Gas/Oil ONLY Field Translation: Wood fires get incredibly hot and produce corrosive creosote. If you use a cheap 304 or AL29-4C liner for a wood stove, it will literally burn through. Always use 316 for solid fuels.
Liner Insulation
Required for UL listing		 1/2" Foil-Faced Wrap (Min) OR Pour-down mix (Thermix) Field Translation: If the outside of the masonry chimney touches wood framing (zero clearance), you MUST wrap the liner or pour insulation around it. Otherwise, heat transfers through the brick and can start a house fire.
🏠 Clearances & Terminations
The "3-2-10" Rule
Chimney Termination Height		 Min 3 ft above roof penetration Min 2 ft higher than any structure within 10 ft Field Translation: Measure 10 feet horizontally outward from the chimney. The chimney top must be at least 2 feet taller than anything inside that 10-foot circle (like the roof peak, a dormer, or a wall).
Clearance to Combustibles
Masonry Chimneys		 Min 2" (Interior Chimneys) Min 1" (Exterior Chimneys) Field Translation: Drywall, wood studs, and siding cannot touch the chimney brick directly. There must be an air gap between the masonry and the house materials.
Masonry & Hearths
🧱 Masonry, Crowns & Hearths
Chimney Crown (Cast/Poured)
Top seal protection (IRC)		 Min 2" thickness at thinnest edge Min 2" overhang past brick w/ drip edge Must have bond break around flue Field Translation: Do not pour the concrete crown tight against the clay flue. Wrap the flue with expansion joint material first. When the flue gets hot and expands, it will crack the concrete crown if there is no bond break to absorb the swelling.
Refractory Mortar (ASTM C199)
Fireboxes and clay flues		 Must use Non-Water Soluble type Max joint thickness: 1/8" to 1/4" Field Translation: Never use standard brick mortar inside a firebox. The intense heat will destroy it. Use pre-mixed refractory cement (like ChamberTech or similar) that can withstand 2,000°F.
Hearth Extension: Small
Firebox Opening < 6 sq. ft.		 Min 16" to the front Min 8" to each side Field Translation: The non-combustible floor (tile/stone) must stick out at least 16 inches in front of the fireplace opening to catch popping embers so they don't burn the carpet or hardwood.
Hearth Extension: Large
Firebox Opening ≥ 6 sq. ft.		 Min 20" to the front Min 12" to each side
Safety & Weatherproofing
💧 Flashing & Fall Protection
Cricket / Saddle Rules
Diverting water behind chimney		 Required if chimney is > 30" wide Field Translation: If the back of the chimney facing the roof peak is wider than 30 inches, it acts like a dam for rain and snow. You must build a small peaked roof (cricket) behind it to shed the water to the sides.
Fall Protection (OSHA)
When harnesses are required		 Any work at or above 6 feet Field Translation: If your boots are 6 feet or higher off the ground, OSHA requires you to be wearing a harness tied off to a roof anchor (Personal Fall Arrest System - PFAS).
Ladder Extensions
Roof access protocols		 Must extend 3 ft above landing 4:1 Angle Rule Field Translation: The top of the ladder must stick up 3 rungs past the gutter. For the angle: for every 4 feet of ladder height, pull the base of the ladder 1 foot away from the wall.
