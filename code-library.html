🕵️ Chimney Fire Forensics
NFPA 211 - 14.5
Visual Evidence of Hostile Fire Puffed, honeycomb-like creosote Vertical "clean" cracks in clay tiles Warped damper plates / discolored caps
📖 Read the "Why"

The Reality: Most chimney fires go unnoticed by the homeowner. The extreme heat instantly turns flat creosote into expanded honeycomb and creates massive, vertical, razor-thin cracks (Thermal Shock).
🚫 Defect Limits & Failure Criteria
NFPA 211 - 14.4.1
Creosote Buildup Sweep if > 1/8" thick Fails if Stage 3 (Glaze) is present
NFPA 211 - 14.9
Flue Liner Integrity Zero tolerance for cracks/splits Fails if ANY tile is cracked
☢️ Hazmat & Asbestos
Asbestos Protocol
Transite Pipe & Rope Gaskets White/gray cement-like gas vents (Transite) Frayed white rope gaskets on old inserts DO NOT SWEEP OR DISTURB
📖 Read the "Why"

The Danger: Transite is an asbestos-cement pipe common in pre-1980s gas vents. If you brush it, you release friable asbestos fibers into the home and your lungs. If you see white, chalky pipe or fibrous white rope seals falling apart, halt service immediately.
