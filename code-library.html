🔍 Inspection Levels & Triggers
NFPA 211 - 15.3.1
Level 1 Inspection
Readily accessible areas Routine annual maintenance Same appliance, same fuel
📖 Read the "Why"

The Reality: A Level 1 is a basic visual check of the portions of the chimney you can see without moving anything. "Readily accessible" means no tools required.
NFPA 211 - 15.4.1
Level 2 Inspection
Accessible + Camera Scan Sale/Transfer of property Post-chimney fire or weather event Mandatory camera scan
📖 Read the "Why"

The Liability: If a house was just sold, had a chimney fire, or is getting a new fuel type, the code mandates a Level 2. You must run a camera up the flue. Document the scan to protect EverSafe's liability.

📸 REQUIRED PHOTOS: 1x Wide shot of property exterior, 1x Wide shot of appliance, Full video link to camera scan.
🚫 Defect Limits & Failure Criteria
NFPA 211 - 14.4.1
Creosote Buildup Sweep if > 1/8" thick Fails if Stage 3 (Glaze) is present
📖 Read the "Why"

The Danger: Stage 3 expands up to 7 times its size when ignited, blocking the flue and pushing a 2000-degree blowtorch into the living room. Quote a chemical rotary treatment (PCR).

📸 REQUIRED PHOTOS: 1x Flash photo up the flue showing glaze, 1x Close up of damper frame buildup.
NFPA 211 - 14.9
Flue Liner Integrity Zero tolerance for cracks/splits Fails if ANY tile is cracked
📖 Read the "Why"

The Danger: Clay tiles fail due to thermal shock. Even a hairline crack breaks the seal, allowing heat and carbon monoxide to escape into the home's wood framing. Fail the flue and quote a stainless liner.

📸 REQUIRED PHOTOS: 1x Clear screenshot from camera scan showing the specific crack/void.
☔ Waterproofing & Preservation
ASTM E514
Masonry Water Repellents Must be 100% vapor permeable Fails if painted or film-sealed
Crown Repair
Elastomeric Crown Coatings Used for cracks < 1/8" wide Rebuild required if structurally compromised
📖 Read the "Why"

The Rule: Brushable sealants (CrownCoat) preserve a crown. However, if the concrete is loose, crumbling, or cracks exceed 1/8", it is a structural failure. It must be torn off and re-poured.

📸 REQUIRED PHOTOS: 1x Wide shot of crown, 1x Macro close-up of crack with tape measure/coin for scale.

NFPA 211 - 11.2.3.3
Combustible Trim & Mantels Min 6" from opening for 1.5" projection Fails if TV mounted in radiant heat zone
📖 Read the "Why"

The Physics: Heat rolls out and up from the firebox. A deep wood mantel catches that rolling heat. The deeper the mantel sticks out from the wall, the higher up it must be mounted to avoid bursting into flames.

🏭 Prefab Systems & Ash Pits
UL 127
Factory-Built Fireplaces (Zero-Clearance) Must use OEM replacement parts Fails if parts are mixed & matched
📖 Read the "Why"

The Liability: A prefab fireplace is tested as a complete assembly. If you connect Brand X pipe to a Brand Y firebox, you instantly void the UL listing. Always order OEM parts.

📸 REQUIRED PHOTOS: 1x Clear shot of the metal data tag (usually inside the side rails).

IMC 504.8.4
Maximum Duct Length Max 35 feet (Concealed) Deduct 5ft for every 90° elbow
📖 Read the "Why"

The Physics: Dryers only have enough blower power to push heavy, wet lint 35 feet. Every time the pipe turns 90 degrees, it destroys blower pressure. If the run is too long, lint settles in the pipe, cooks, and catches fire.
IMC 504.8.1
Materials & Fasteners Rigid, smooth metal duct only (4") NO SCREWS allowed in joints
🏠 Roof Terminations
IRC R1003.9
The "3-2-10" Rule Min 3 ft above roof penetration Min 2 ft higher than structure within 10 ft
📖 View Diagram & Explanation

Measure 10 feet out. The chimney must be built at least 2 feet taller than anything in that circle.
