🧹 Equipment & Tool Standards
OEM Warranty Specs
Stainless Steel Liners (UL 1777) Poly brushes / Nylon Whips ONLY NEVER use steel wire brushes
📖 Read the "Why"

The Physics: Stainless steel builds a microscopic layer of oxidation that prevents rust. A hard carbon-steel wire brush digs into the titanium alloy, transferring rustable carbon particles into the scratches. It instantly voids the $2,000 lifetime liner warranty.
Industry Best Practice
Terra Cotta Clay Flues Wire brushes or Chain Rotary Systems Poly brushes ineffective on Stage 2 creosote
🚨 Carbon Monoxide (CO) Limits
OSHA / EPA / UL Limits
Actionable PPM Levels (Parts Per Million) 0 - 9 PPM: Normal / ASHRAE Max Allowable 35 PPM: EPA 1-Hour Max Exposure 70 PPM: UL Alarm Threshold / Symptoms 200+ PPM: LETHAL / EVACUATE IMMEDIATELY
📖 Read the "Why"

The Protocol: If your personal monitor hits 35 PPM while testing a gas appliance, crack a window and advise the client. If it hits 70 PPM or above, the appliance is failing catastrophically. Shut the gas off, red-tag the unit, and ventilate the house.
