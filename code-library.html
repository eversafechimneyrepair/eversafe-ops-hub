Building Code Library

Comprehensive reference guide for structural, electrical, life safety, and accessibility standards in the field.

Detailed Scopes
🏗️ 1. Structural & General Building (IBC & IRC)
  • Load Requirements: Local wind speed ratings (crucial for hurricane zones), snow loads, and seismic design categories.
  • Foundation & Anchoring: Concrete pad thickness specifications, anchor bolt sizing, and required embedment depths.
  • Material Standards: Steel gauge requirements, structural integrity of framing, and span limits.
⚡ 2. Electrical & Backup Power (NEC / NFPA 70)
  • Low-Voltage (Class 2/3): Wiring standards for access control, cameras, and automated gates.
  • High-Voltage & Power: Generator transfer switches (ATS), load calculations, and conduit routing.
  • Grounding & Bonding: Protection requirements for metal structures and electrical systems against lightning and faults.
🔥 3. Fire & Life Safety (IFC & NFPA 101)
  • Means of Egress: Allowable door hardware, panic bars, and emergency push panels on security doors.
  • Fire Resistance Ratings: Rules for fire-stopping materials when penetrating fire-rated walls with conduits, cables, or structural anchors.
  • Clearances: Maintaining required distances around electrical panels, heating equipment, and fire alarm strobes.
🛡️ 4. Security & Ballistics (UL & ASTM)
  • Ballistic Standards: UL 752 levels (e.g., Level 1 through Level 3 ratings for panels and glass).
  • Forced Entry: ASTM standards for security screen mesh, main frame extrusions, and hinge requirements.
  • Glazing & Laminate: Wind-borne debris impact standards and peel/tensile strength codes for security films.
♿ 5. Accessibility (ADA & ICC A117.1)
  • Mounting Heights: Exact allowable heights for card readers, access panels, and door handles.
  • Clearances: Required maneuvering clearance around doors, entryways, and gates.
  • Protrusion Limits: Rules on how far a screen, enclosure, or piece of hardware can stick out into a hallway or path without being a hazard.
🍃 6. Energy & Environmental (IECC)
  • Insulation & Thermal: R-value requirements for roofs and walls.
  • Vapor & Weather Barriers: Requirements for sealing joints, flashing, and managing moisture.
