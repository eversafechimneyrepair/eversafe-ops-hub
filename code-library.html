🚨 Carbon Monoxide (CO) Limits
OSHA / EPA / UL Limits
Actionable PPM Levels (Parts Per Million) 0 - 9 PPM: Normal / ASHRAE Max Allowable 35 PPM: EPA 1-Hour Max Exposure 70 PPM: UL Alarm Threshold / Symptoms 200+ PPM: LETHAL / EVACUATE IMMEDIATELY
📖 Read the "Why"

The Protocol: If your personal monitor hits 35 PPM while testing a gas appliance, crack a window and advise the client. If it hits 70 PPM or above, the appliance is failing catastrophically. Shut the gas off, red-tag the unit, and ventilate the house. Extended exposure at 70 PPM causes headaches/nausea. 400 PPM causes death within hours.

💨 Venting & HVAC Categories
NFPA 54 / IFGC
Appliance Venting Categories Cat I (Non-Condensing, Negative Draft): B-Vent / 304 / Clay Cat IV (Condensing, Positive): PVC / AL29-4C ONLY
📖 Read the "Why"

The Physics: Modern 90%+ efficient furnaces (Category IV) extract so much heat from the exhaust that what comes out of the pipe is literally highly acidic water vapor under pressure. If a plumber vents this into a cold brick chimney, the acid water eats the mortar from the inside out in months. Never let Cat IV vent into clay or standard 316 stainless.
