🔍 Inspection Levels & Triggers
NFPA 211 - 15.3.1
Level 1 Inspection
Readily accessible areas Routine annual maintenance Same appliance, same fuel
NFPA 211 - 15.4.1
Level 2 Inspection
Accessible + Camera Scan Sale/Transfer of property Post-chimney fire or weather event Mandatory camera scan
📖 Read the "Why"

The Liability: If a house was just sold, had a chimney fire, or is getting a new fuel type, the code mandates a Level 2. You must run a camera up the flue.

📸 REQUIRED PHOTOS: 1x Wide shot exterior, 1x Wide shot appliance, Video link to scan.
🚫 Defect Limits & Failure Criteria
NFPA 211 - 14.4.1
Creosote Buildup Sweep if > 1/8" thick Fails if Stage 3 (Glaze) is present
📖 Read the "Why"

The Danger: Stage 3 expands up to 7 times its size when ignited, blocking the flue and pushing a 2000-degree blowtorch into the living room.

📸 REQUIRED PHOTOS: 1x Flash photo up the flue showing glaze, 1x Close up of damper frame buildup.
NFPA 211 - 14.9
Flue Liner Integrity Zero tolerance for cracks/splits Fails if ANY tile is cracked
📖 Read the "Why"

The Danger: Clay tiles fail due to thermal shock. Even a hairline crack breaks the seal, allowing heat and carbon monoxide to escape into the home's wood framing.

📸 REQUIRED PHOTOS: 1x Clear screenshot from camera scan showing the specific crack/void.
IRC R1003.8
Smoke Chamber Parging Must be parged smooth Fails if brick steps are exposed
☔ Waterproofing & Preservation
ASTM E514
Masonry Water Repellents Must be 100% vapor permeable Fails if painted or film-sealed
Crown Repair
Elastomeric Crown Coatings Used for cracks < 1/8" wide Rebuild required if structurally compromised
📖 Read the "Why"

The Rule: Brushable sealants (CrownCoat) preserve a crown. However, if the concrete is loose, crumbling, or cracks exceed 1/8", it is a structural failure. It must be torn off and re-poured.

📸 REQUIRED PHOTOS: 1x Wide shot of crown, 1x Macro close-up of crack with tape measure/coin for scale.
🧪 Chemical App & Weather Limits
Siloxane Repellent
Water Repellent Application Min Temp: 40°F (and rising) Rain Limit: Requires 6 dry hours
Elastomeric Sealants
CrownCoat / FlashSeal Application Min Temp: 45°F Rain Limit: Requires 12-24 dry hours
Poultice / PCR
Creosote Remover (PCR) Min Temp: 50°F ambient flue temp Cure Time: 24 to 48 hours to dry
📖 Read the "Why"

The Physics: PCR must dry completely to fracture the glaze creosote. If you apply it when the outside air is 30 degrees, the flue is too cold, the water in the product freezes, and it will never dry and flake off.
